Number Theory Algorithm

Euclidean GCD

Beginner

Computes the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of two numbers using repeated division. Replace the larger number with the remainder until the remainder is zero. The last non-zero remainder is the GCD.

Best: O(1)
Average: O(log min(a,b))
Worst: O(log min(a,b))
Space: O(1)
🔄
Remainder Operation
Repeatedly replace a with b and b with a % b.
📉
Reduction
Numbers get smaller each step, ensuring quick convergence.
⚡
Efficient
Worst case is logarithmic in the smaller number.
📚
Ancient Algorithm
Described by Euclid around 300 BC.
